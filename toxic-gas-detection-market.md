<p><p>The toxic gas detection market is categorized into various technologies: </p><p>1. **Laser**: Utilizes laser beams to detect gases by measuring light absorption, ideal for long-range detection. </p><p>2. **Infrared**: Employs infrared spectroscopy to identify gases based on their unique absorption wavelengths, effective for hydrocarbons and other organic compounds. </p><p>3. **Electrochemical**: Involves sensors that produce an electrical current when exposed to specific gases, commonly used for detecting toxic and combustible gases. </p><p>4. **Others**: This category includes technologies like metal oxide semiconductors and photoionization detectors, catering to niche applications and diverse environments.</p></p>
