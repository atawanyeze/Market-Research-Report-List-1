<p><p>The Oilfield Surfactants market consists of four main types: Anionic Surfactants, which carry a negative charge and are effective in reducing interfacial tension and improving oil recovery; Non-Ionic Surfactants, which lack any electrical charge, offering stability across various conditions; Cationic Surfactants, positively charged and used for their antimicrobial properties; and Amphoteric Surfactants, which can act as either cationic or anionic depending on the environment, providing versatility in applications like drilling, enhanced oil recovery, and environmental management.</p></p>
