<p><p>The prophylactic human vaccine market comprises several types, primarily inactivated vaccines, attenuated vaccines, and others. Inactivated vaccines contain killed pathogens, providing immunity without causing disease. Attenuated vaccines use weakened pathogens to stimulate a robust immune response. Other types include subunit, conjugate, and mRNA vaccines, each targeting specific components of pathogens to elicit immunity. These diverse vaccine types cater to various infectious diseases, enhancing public health through effective prevention strategies. Together, they contribute significantly to global vaccination efforts.</p></p>
