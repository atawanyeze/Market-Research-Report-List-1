<p><p>The glasses-free 3D TV market primarily features two types: active and passive 3D TVs. Active 3D TVs use electronic shutters in glasses to synchronize with the display, offering high-quality images but requiring glasses. In contrast, passive 3D TVs utilize polarized images that don't require glasses, providing a more comfortable viewing experience but at a lower resolution. Both technologies aim to enhance viewer engagement, with active TVs focusing on clarity and depth, while passive options emphasize accessibility and ease of use.</p></p>
