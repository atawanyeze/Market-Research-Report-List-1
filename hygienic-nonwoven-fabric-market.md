<p><strong>The Hygienic Nonwoven Fabric Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Spunlaced</li><li>Heat Sealing</li><li>Dry Papermaking</li><li>Meltblown</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The hygienic nonwoven fabric market comprises several key types: </p><p>1. **Spunlaced**: Produced by entangling fibers with high-pressure water jets, offering soft and flexible materials ideal for medical and personal care products. </p><p>2. **Heat Sealing**: Uses heat to bond fibers, providing strength and durability for various applications, including diapers and feminine hygiene products. </p><p>3. **Dry Papermaking**: Involves forming sheets through dry processes, resulting in lightweight but sturdy fabrics, mainly for hygiene applications. </p><p>4. **Meltblown**: Consists of fine, thermally bonded fibers, known for filtering and absorption, used in masks and wipes. </p><p>5. **Other**: Includes diverse methods and innovations enhancing functionality and performance tailored to specific hygienic needs.</p></p>
