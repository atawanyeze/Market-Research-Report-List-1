<p><p>The bio-surfactants market is categorized into three primary types: anionic, cationic, and amphoteric surfactants. Anionic surfactants possess a negative charge, making them effective in emulsification and detergency. Cationic surfactants, carrying a positive charge, are known for their antimicrobial properties and are often used in personal care products. Amphoteric surfactants can act as both anionic and cationic, providing versatility in applications spanning from cosmetics to industrial cleaning. Each type plays a vital role in sustainable formulations across various industries.</p></p>
