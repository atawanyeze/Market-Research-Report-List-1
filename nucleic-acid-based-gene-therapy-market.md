<p><p>Nucleic Acid Based Gene Therapy encompasses various market types targeting genetic diseases. Anti-sense and anti-gene therapies inhibit specific gene expression, while short inhibitory sequences (siRNA) facilitate RNA interference to downregulate target genes. Gene transfer therapy involves delivering therapeutic genes using vectors. Nucleoside analogs mimic natural nucleosides, disrupting viral replication. Ribozymes are catalytic RNA molecules that cleave specific RNA targets, and aptamers are RNA or DNA molecules that bind tightly to proteins. Other emerging technologies continue to diversify the market.</p></p>
